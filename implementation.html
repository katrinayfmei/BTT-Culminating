Implementation


The trees will be made in Canada then shipped to Pakistan. There they will be assembled by volunteers. Each treepod is 6 meters tall and 10 meters wide. The trees will be spread approximately 15 meters apart. To implement our project we will be using 4500m or 4.5 km squared of space. This is the perfect amount of space to rebuild a forest and is large enough for the community to gather peacfully. There will be a grand opening, where the community will be invited to test out the equipment, get a feel for the space, talk, mingle, play, learn and listen to music. This will help the community adjust to the new area. We will also hold weekly activity sessions for the first six months after the projects opening. There is a month long, rotating schedules of activities to help the citizens integrate with each other and us. The schedule includes cards, board game cafe, movie/play viewing and yoga. Reforestation knows from experience that this is an effective way to engage the community and smoothly implemented our Treepods. Our hope is that as the project proves successful and a relationship is formed with the Pakistani government we will be able to expand our project. Helping communities around the world regrow their forests, reduce their carbon dioxide and obtain peace.

Cost


Each tree cost $20,000. Factoring in costs such as shipping, benches and saplings Reforestnation will be asking for $500,000 to help start up our company. Other costs we nticipate are advertisements and other utilities. Advertising is an important part of an organization's launch because it helps them gain donations, volunteers, support and other additional help. $500,000 is not a large price to pay for safe communities, a clean environment and a peaceful world.

Anticipated Problems and Solutions

Problem

Getting Started

We are unsure willingness of the community to help out and take time out of their day to grow and tend to the forest being planted

Solution

The One Year Plan

We Get Our Hands Dirty Too

In the first year of the project we will stay in Pakistan to watch over the space. We will go to schools and talk to community members, educating and sparking interest in our project

Problem

Tracking Projects Growth

Tracking the project's progress given the distance between Canada and Pakistan is a large concern.

Solution

Volunteers

We Help Them and They Help You

We will have volunteers watching over our Treepods at all times. They will keep the space clean and help the community regrow their forest. Volunteers will also ensure that we know what is happening at all times.

Problem

Weather

Pakistan is prone to major flooding which could potentially destroy the very expensive treepods and wreck the progress or the new forest.

Solution

Staying Alert

A Future Solution

Our most concerning problem is the weather because we cannot change it, but we can track it. Reforestnation will be keeping tabs on the weather and as our project becomes more stable we will figure out a simple solution such as building a sandbag barrier to prevent severe weather damage.

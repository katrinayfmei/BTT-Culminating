REFORESTNATION PROJECT
Pakistan




Project Summary

We will start our project in the capital city of the southern province of Sindh, Karachi. We will purchase 20 Treepods and place them in a smaller community within Karachi called Sultanabad. We will provide the community with seating where citizens can peacefully communicate and light so they can gather safely. We will also provide saplings for people to plant. Our plan is for community members to plant and regrow a forest while our Treepods are absorbing CO2. Once a forest has begun to grow we will move our trees and begin the process again with a new community.

    Why Pakistan?

    Pakistan is our very first project.There are two reasons we choose to start our project in Pakistan. Firstly, Pakistan has frequent domestic and foreign conflicts. In the country, it is dangerous for children and women as they get abducted and assaulted frequently. Pakistan has been involved in worldwide conflicts. In recent years India and Pakistan tensions have been rising over disputed land in Kashmir. We should pay attention to Pakistan and India as both countries have nuclear weapons and can potentially start a nuclear war.

    The second reason we choose Pakistan is because of there environmental problems. Pakistan is the 7th most vulnerable country to climate change and has already suffered from climate change like extreme heat. Climate change has threatened Pakistan's water and food supply affecting citizens throughout the country. Another problem is many trees have been destroyed, due to previous conflicts. Pakistan had frequent terrorist attacks meaning most of the forests were destroyed. Pakistan also has a huge air pollution problem. Most of the air pollution in Pakistan is coming from crop burning which releases CO2 in the atmosphere. Thousands of lives have been already from air pollution. Pakistan does not have many trees meaning there is nothing to clean the air.

    Luckily Pakistan is recognizing these problems and is willing to change. Pakistan has many tree planting initiatives like the Billion Tree Tsunami. In 2016, Pakistan partnered with the UN to implement the Sustainable Development Goals to the curriculum. Pakistan is making changes to improve their education, over half of their population is under 30 years old. Providing better education can improve the countries economy and decrease the amount of violence in the country. The new generation can transition the country to be clean. safe and sustainable.

Why Should We Care For The Environment?

Today, there many environmental problems occurring on earth. If we do not take care of the earth we are the ones who suffer. At Reforestnation, our goal is to create peace and save the earth. There are many issues in the environment but, here are some problems we are trying to solve:

  • Air Pollution
  • Climate Change
  • Deforestation
    •
    Air Pollution
    There are 7 million premature deaths each year due to air pollution. -Estimation by The World Health Organization

    Air pollution is happening worldwide and is causing many problems . Air pollution causes health problems. In a short duration, people may suffer from headaches and irritation in the nose or eye. The long terms effects include heart disease and lung cancer. Air pollution can be caused by man-made sources, natural sources and emissions of vehicles. Air pollution effects for wildlife, animals are just like humans. Animals can experience health problems as well. Smog also reduces visibility for both humans and animals. Plants and trees are affected as well. Water and soil can be contaminated causing plants and trees to die at a young age.

    Climate Change

    Climate change is also known as global warming. Climate change is when we as human have released too much carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases that the earth is warming up. As a result, we are seeing countries worldwide suffering from extreme weather. If you want to learn more about climate change click on the video above! Many countries are planting trees worldwide. the earth already has too much carbon in the atmosphere, by planting trees they absorb access carbon and release oxygen.

    Deforestation

    Deforestation is the act of clearing large lands of trees. It is a global problem that causes climate change and desertification. People cut down trees for many reasons like to get resources like oil, clearing land for industrial use and can be used to cut down resources in war. Deforestation affects many things in an ecosystem, by cutting down trees we destroy the habitats of animals. Trees absorb carbon and by cutting down trees we emit more carbon into the atmosphere. The rate of deforestation continues to grow.

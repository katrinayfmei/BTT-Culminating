FAQs

Below are some questions that we have frequently been asked. If you have any other questions or inquries, please feel free to message us on social media, shoot us an email or send us a letter.


Who do you partner with?

WeforestNATION has partnered with many organisations and governments around the world, such as the United Nations, the government of Canada and the government of Pakistan.
How do you generate funds?

WeforestNATION generates funds through donations from people like you. No donation is too large or too small, and every bit makes a difference. We have also received a $500,000 grant from generous entrepreneurs.
How do I donate?

You can donate by clicking here! Donations come through GoFundMe or can be delivered in person to our heardquarters in Toronto, ON.
Do I need an application to volunteer?

Yes, you need an eligible application to volunteer. Applicants must be over the age of 18, have proper health insurance and legal citizenship. For more information on the conditions to apply, or to obtain an application form, visit our volunteer page here.
What are donations used for?

100% of the donations go towards funding the Treepods and creating a safer, healthier, more eco-friendly community. None of the money we receive from donations goes into our pockets, and we appreciate each and every person who takes the time to help us out. Help create a more sustainable future by clicking here.
Why do we need Treepods?

Treepods are our solution to a global problem that everyone contributes to. Worldwide, climate change has many different effects, one of them being a decline in proper air quality. Poor air quality can cause disease and harm many people, especially where smog is the most present, such as in Pakistan. If we do not deduce our carbon dioxide emissions by 45% within the next 12 years, there is no going back. We need Treepods because everyone must do their part to keep the Earth healthy and alive. Watch the following video to learn more about deforestation and pollution in specific areas.

How do Treepods work?

Treepods use humidity swing technology, a technology that was first invented by Klaus Lackner and Allen Wright. The material they designed absorbs carbon dioxide when it's dry, and releases it when it's wet. This material is part of the branches of the Treepods and sucks carbon dioxide from the air. The carbon dioxide goes through a filtration systen powered by solar panels and is then stored underground. For more information and details on our technology, visit our tech page here.
How much Co2 does a Treepod collect?

1 Treepod can collect as much carbon dioxide as 100 natural trees. For more facts and details on out Treepods, visit our tech page here.
